Rome

Echoes of an Empire

Rome’s most iconic monument, the Colosseum, was built between 70 and 80 AD and could seat up to 50,000 spectators for gladiatorial contests and animal hunts. Nearby, the Palatine Hill preserves ruins of imperial palaces on the very site where legend says Romulus founded the city.

Sacred Spaces

Across the Tiber, St Peter’s Basilica anchors the Vatican. It houses Michelangelo’s delicate Pietà and remains the spiritual heart of the Catholic Church. In the centre of Rome, the Pantheon boasts perfect symmetry: its domed temple built in 126 AD has inspired architects for millennia.

Palaces, Galleries & Tombs

The Baroque Palazzo Barberini welcomes visitors with opulent staircases, fountains and an art gallery featuring masters of the 17th century. Beneath the city, the labyrinthine Catacombs stretch for hundreds of kilometres, preserving frescoed burial chambers carved by early Christians.

The imposing white Altare della Patria, built after Italy’s unification, symbolizes national unity and hosts official ceremonies. Its rooftop terraces offer sweeping views over Rome’s historic centre.
