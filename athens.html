Athens

The Sacred Rock

The heart of Athens is the Acropolis, a citadel rising above the city. Its crowning temple, the Parthenon, was dedicated to the goddess Athena around 447 BCE. Nearby stand the Temple of Athena Nike, built around 420 BCE, and the Erechtheion, known for the graceful Caryatids that support its porch. At the foot of the hill lies the Odeon of Herodes Atticus, a stone theatre built in 161 CE that still hosts concerts today. The Acropolis and its monuments are designated as UNESCO World Heritage for their enduring influence on architecture and democracy.

Ancient & Classical Sites

Beyond the Acropolis, Athens brims with classical heritage. The National Archaeological Museum houses Greece’s most comprehensive collection of antiquities. Day trips take you to the UNESCO‑listed sanctuary of Delphi on Mount Parnassus, revered in antiquity as the centre of the world. At the tip of Cape Sounion stands the Temple of Poseidon, a 5th‑century marvel overlooking the Aegean.

In the heart of the old city lies the Ancient Agora, a gathering place of philosophers and citizens. Nearby, the colossal Temple of Olympian Zeus features Corinthian columns and was one of the largest temples of the ancient world.
