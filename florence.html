Florence

The Cradle of the Renaissance

Florence’s skyline is dominated by the Duomo, the city’s cathedral whose marble façade and terracotta dome blend Gothic and Renaissance artistry. Visitors can climb Giotto’s bell tower and Brunelleschi’s dome for sweeping views, while inside frescoes narrate the city’s cultural legacy.

Nearby, the Basilica of Santa Croce houses the tombs of Michelangelo and Galileo within its soaring Gothic interior. Just across the river, the Ponte Vecchio spans the Arno with shops lining a two‑storey gallery. Built in 1345, it is the first segmental arch bridge in the West and was the only Florentine bridge spared during World War II.

Gardens & Galleries

The Boboli Gardens behind Palazzo Pitti offer a green refuge with Renaissance sculptures, fountains and an open‑air theatre spread over 111 acres. Inside the palace, the Palatine Gallery exhibits works by Raphael and Titian. Across town, the Accademia Gallery displays Michelangelo’s David, while the Uffizi Gallery houses masterpieces by Botticelli, Leonardo da Vinci and Caravaggio.

Overlooking Piazza della Signoria, the fortified Palazzo Vecchio stands as Florence’s historic town hall and a symbol of civic power.
