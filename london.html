London

Modern Views

The London Eye rises 135 metres above the River Thames, providing panoramic views of the city’s landmarks—including Big Ben, the Houses of Parliament, Buckingham Palace, The Shard and St Paul’s Cathedral. Each capsule on the wheel represents a London borough.

Towers & Fortresses

On the north bank stands the Tower of London, a former royal residence, prison and treasury now housing the Crown Jewels and exhibitions on a thousand years of history. Downriver, the Tower Bridge—a late‑Victorian bascule and suspension bridge—was built between 1886 and 1894 with twin towers 61 metres tall. Visitors can walk its glass‑floored high level walkways and view the original steam engine rooms.

Palaces & Cathedrals

The grand Buckingham Palace began life as the Duke of Buckingham’s townhouse in 1703. It has served as the London residence of the British monarch since 1837 and contains 775 rooms; parts of the palace open to visitors each summer.

Designed by Sir Christopher Wren after the Great Fire of 1666, St Paul’s Cathedral features a majestic limestone façade and a dome rising 365 feet. The 300‑year‑old cathedral stands as a monument to resilience and architectural ingenuity.
